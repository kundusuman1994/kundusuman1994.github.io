I work on the analytic structure of quantum field theory and gravity — scattering amplitudes and their Regge growth, holography and AdS black holes, and the string theory dual of two-dimensional Yang–Mills.

My research asks what consistency — causality, unitarity, and bounds on chaos — demands of a theory of gravity and its holographic description. Recent work includes an explicit worldsheet string dual for 2D Yang–Mills (with Ofer Aharony and Tal Sheaffer), the “Grey Galaxies” endpoint of the Kerr–AdS superradiant instability, and the flat-space limit of the S-matrix and its boundary correlators.

Before joining IIT Palakkad I was a postdoctoral fellow at SISSA, Trieste, and a Koshland Fellow at the Weizmann Institute. I completed my PhD at TIFR Mumbai under Shiraz Minwalla.

Research

Research summary

My work centres on the constraints that consistency — causality, unitarity, and chaos bounds — places on theories of gravity and their holographic descriptions.

Scattering amplitudes & the Regge limit

I study how bounds on chaos in holographic CFTs translate into bounds on the Regge growth of flat-space scattering amplitudes — the Chaos–Regge Growth (CRG) statement — and how causality constrains local four-point amplitudes of massive particles with spin, connecting to swampland criteria for isolated massive spin-2 states.

The string dual of 2D Yang–Mills

With Ofer Aharony and Tal Sheaffer I have been constructing an explicit worldsheet string theory whose partition function reproduces two-dimensional Yang–Mills theory, giving a concrete, solvable instance of gauge–string duality.

AdS black holes & holography

I proposed “Grey Galaxies” as an endpoint of the Kerr–AdS superradiant instability, and study CFT correlators on the Lorentzian cylinder, the flat-space limit of the S-matrix and its boundary correlators, and cosmological correlators in de Sitter α-vacua.

Lecture series

Physics Beyond the Classroom — A Few Puzzles in Physics
Presidency University Physics Society · 6 lectures × 1 hour · online · from 23 June 2026

A six-lecture public series tracing the four great frameworks of modern physics — special relativity, general relativity, quantum field theory, and the road to quantum gravity — through the specific puzzles that forced each one into being. Every lecture opens with a contradiction the existing theory could not resolve and closes with the conceptual structure physicists were forced to adopt. The final lecture reaches one of the deepest open problems today: the tension between general relativity and quantum theory, and what string theory and holography have to say about it.

  1. Puzzles leading to EM & special relativity — Maxwell, Michelson–Morley, and the Lorentz transformation.
  2. Puzzles leading to general relativity — the equivalence principle and dynamical spacetime.
  3. Consequences of general relativity — light bending, gravitational waves, black holes, cosmology.
  4. The need for quantum field theory — blackbody radiation, antiparticles, and causality.
  5. Consequences of QFT — QED, the electron g−2, gauge symmetry, and the LHC.
  6. A current puzzle — quantising gravity, the black-hole information paradox, string theory, holography.
